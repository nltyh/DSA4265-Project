### 2. KEY RISK SIGNALS
**Geopolitics & Policy | Geopolitical Conflict | Severity: High**  
- **Headline:** Trump cuts financial lifeline for Venezuela by ending permit to export oil to U.S.

- **Market impact:** This decision may tighten the supply of Venezuelan oil available to the U.S. and could disadvantage the Venezuelan economy further, exerting upward pressure on crude oil prices.  
- **Sentiment contribution:** Bullish — as reduced supply from Venezuela may lead to higher prices.

- **Headline:** Oil falls on White House spat, tariffs, Iraq exports  
- **What happened:** Oil prices fell due to a disagreement between U.S. and Ukrainian leaders and the resumption of oil exports from the Kurdistan region of Iraq.

- **Sentiment contribution:** Bearish — due to factors that may lead to a reduction in oil prices.

**Financial Flows / Positioning | No Significant Risk | Severity: Low**  
- **Headline:** Petrobras to issue dividends despite fourth quarter net loss of $2.8 billion

- **Sentiment contribution:** Neutral — as this does not significantly affect the broader crude oil market outlook.

**Geopolitics & Policy | Regulatory Constraint | Severity: Medium**  
- **Headline:** BP to Focus More on Oil and Gas in Strategy ‘Reset’  
- **What happened:** BP announced a shift back to oil and gas, cutting investments in renewable energy significantly, which suggests a retreat from the energy transition agenda.  
- **Market impact:** This may indicate a tightening in oil supply in the future as companies pull away from renewable projects amid demands for higher returns.  
- **Sentiment contribution:** Bullish — an increased focus on oil may tighten future supply.

---
### 3. SIGNAL SYNTHESIS
The signals indicate aligned concerns regarding geopolitical tensions stemming from U.S. sanctions against Venezuela, which could tighten supply. The prevailing bearish sentiment from drops in oil prices amidst geopolitical conflicts and regulatory shifts represents a complex risk environment.
