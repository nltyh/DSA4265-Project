The increased demand for LNG in Europe has diverted shipments from Asia, creating energy shortages and potential price increases in the Asian market. The ongoing adjustments indicate an upward trend in prices.

- **Headline:** Europe’s scramble for LNG leaves Asia starving for energy

The key risk signals are aligned in indicating an overall bullish sentiment for LNG prices due to increased demand and supply chain disruptions stemming from geopolitical factors. While the long-term contracts may provide optimism, immediate market dynamics suggest heightened prices in Asia due to competition for limited supplies.
