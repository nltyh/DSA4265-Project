- **What happened:** Analysts have revised global LNG supply forecasts down by approximately 35 million tons due to production disruptions stemming from the war in the Middle East [Article 1].
- **Market impact:** This shortfall could strain global supply chains and lead to elevated prices as demand continues while availability declines.
- **Sentiment contribution:** Bearish — the reduced supply outlook drives prices upward and affects market stability.

**LNG & natural gas | Supply Chain Blockage | Severity: High**
- **Headline:** Soaring Prices Set to Crash China’s LNG Imports to 8-Year Low
- **What happened:** Due to soaring prices influenced by the war, LNG imports into China are expected to hit their lowest in eight years, with significant supply reductions from Qatar and the UAE [Article 2].

- **Sentiment contribution:** Bearish — this sharp decline in demand signals potential for weakened prices in global markets.

**Geopolitics & policy | Geopolitical Conflict | Severity: High**
- **Headline:** Middle East War Could Push West Canada’s LNG Projects Forward
- **What happened:** The conflict has resulted in a halt of about 20% of global LNG trade flows, potentially accelerating projects in Western Canada [Article 4].
- **Market impact:** While this may improve Canada’s exports, the immediate disruption creates uncertainty in global supply chains.
- **Sentiment contribution:** Neutral — while it opens opportunities for Canadian exports, the destabilization in the Middle East creates overall risk for the market.
